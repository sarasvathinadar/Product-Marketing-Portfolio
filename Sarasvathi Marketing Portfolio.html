Overview

Across my time at Shop101, I wore three distinct hats — each demanding a different skill set, each building on the last. Starting as an Account & Retention Manager overseeing 3,000+ resellers, evolving into an Influencer Marketing Specialist driving app acquisition across 150+ creators, and finally joining the core team that deployed India's first lock screen commerce product during the InMobi acquisition.

Role 1 — Product Launch & GTM Deployment

Roposo Shop: Deploying India's First Lock Screen Commerce Experience

Context

When InMobi acquired Shop101 and launched Roposo Shop — India's first shopping experience embedded on a smartphone's lock screen — it was an entirely new product category with no existing playbook. I was brought into the deployment team to manage influencer selection, onboarding, and all marketing operations across both Shop101 and Roposo during this critical transition window.

Cross-functional execution under pressure

Operating during an active acquisition meant managing two product identities simultaneously. I managed the influencer pipeline for both platforms concurrently, coordinating creator briefs, messaging alignment, and campaign execution without a drop in output quality.

This required rapid context-switching between two distinct GTM narratives while maintaining brand compliance across each — a masterclass in working under ambiguity without losing strategic clarity.

Marketing operations across two brands

My role spanned the full marketing operations remit: influencer selection and vetting, campaign briefing and rollout, performance tracking, and ensuring continuity of growth marketing functions through an organisationally complex period.

What this demonstrates

High-pressure adaptability. Cross-functional agility. The ability to manage product transitions without disrupting market momentum — a core PMM competency.

Context

Shop101 is India's leading reselling platform. My first role here was owning the full lifecycle of 3,000+ active reseller accounts — acquisition, onboarding, training, relationship management, and most critically, revenue retention.

Context & Challenge

A crowded HRTech market with a visibility problem

peopleHum is a B2B HRTech SaaS with strong product capabilities — but the brand wasn't being found by the right buyers at the right moment. In a category dominated by Darwinbox, Keka, and greytHR, they were losing the SERP and LinkedIn positioning battle.

The brief: improve organic visibility, sharpen brand positioning, and generate qualified pipeline.

Target ICP

HR Managers and CHROs at mid-market global companies (200–2,000 employees). LinkedIn-active buyers evaluating HR technology investments.

My Role

Brand positioning + organic demand generation ownership

I owned the brand positioning strategy, content marketing, LinkedIn organic strategy, SEO execution, and GTM planning for webinars — the full top-of-funnel PMM remit for a lean team.

Positioning Approach

Thought Leadership as Category Positioning. In a feature-competitive market, we differentiated peopleHum by becoming the most credible voice in HR thought leadership.

Strategy & Execution

SEO as a competitive positioning weapon

I ran a full competitive analysis of the HRTech SERP landscape using SEMrush and GSC — identifying keyword gaps where peopleHum could realistically rank and win category traffic. Using anchor-text-targeted link exchanges with high-authority sites, I moved the "HR Software" SERP ranking from position 12 to position 6.

Content strategy: I developed content briefs using topic clustering methodology — positioning blog content around ICP pain points rather than product features, building brand authority through editorial credibility.

LinkedIn as a brand positioning channel

For B2B HRTech buyers, LinkedIn is where purchase intent is shaped. I developed a monthly content calendar mixing thought leadership content, social proof, and direct-response posts. This drove a 25% engagement increase in 4 months.

Webinar GTM

I co-led GTM planning for two lead-generating webinars — "Building a Winning HR Strategy" and "Empower Your Organization: Build Your A Team" — owning the promotional strategy, content calendar, and attendee acquisition funnel.

Context & Challenge

SaaS discoverability in the age of AI search

Zoho Recruit and Zoho Forms operate in highly competitive SaaS categories. The challenge wasn't just traditional SEO — it was navigating a fundamentally shifting discovery landscape where AI search engines (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews) are increasingly the first point of contact between a buyer and a product.

The question: How do we ensure Zoho Recruit and Zoho Forms are visible, cited, and positioned correctly in both traditional and AI-driven search environments?

Product ICPs

Zoho Recruit: HR Managers, Talent Acquisition leads, and staffing agencies at SMB/mid-market companies. Zoho Forms: Operations, IT, and marketing teams needing no-code data collection tools.

My Role

Organic visibility strategy across search, AI, and app stores

I owned the multi-channel discoverability strategy for these two products — spanning traditional SEO, a newly-built AI/GEO framework, and full ASO for Android and iOS app properties.

Strategic Framework

Omni-channel Discoverability. As search behaviour fragments across Google, AI assistants, and app stores, product visibility requires a coordinated strategy across all three layers — not optimising each in isolation.

Strategy & Execution

AI-SEO & GEO: A new playbook for LLM visibility

I built Zoho's AI-SEO and Generative Engine Optimisation (GEO) framework from scratch — a playbook for ensuring Zoho products surface in AI-generated answers and are correctly cited in LLM responses.

Key pillars: Brand citation strategy (ensuring Zoho appears in third-party authoritative sources LLMs train on), AI snippet optimisation, and LLM visibility tracking via Profound Analytics. This positioned Zoho ahead of the GEO curve — a channel most SaaS companies are only beginning to formalise in 2024–25.

ASO: Product discoverability on mobile storefronts

I led end-to-end ASO for Zoho Recruit and Zoho Forms on both Android and iOS. Execution: Metadata strategy (title, subtitle, description keyword optimisation), keyword targeting, and performance tracking via MobileAction and Play Console.

Traditional SEO & Reporting

Alongside AI-SEO, I maintained traditional on-page/off-page SEO for new and existing product pages — managing content calendars, tracking ranking trends via GSC and Ahrefs, and delivering weekly performance reports.

Framework: The Three-Layer Discoverability Stack
Layer 1 Traditional Search
On-page, off-page, technical SEO. GSC + Ahrefs + SEMrush tracking.
Layer 2 AI Search (GEO)
LLM citation strategy, AI snippet optimisation, Profound tracking.
Layer 3 App Storefronts (ASO)
Metadata strategy, keyword targeting, Play Console + MobileAction.
Learnings
  • AI search is a positioning channel, not just an SEO tactic. How your product is described by LLMs often mirrors the positioning you've established in high-authority third-party content.
  • ASO is B2B's most underutilised PMM channel. App store keyword optimisation can drive significant organic installs with minimal investment.
  • In SaaS, the product page IS the product experience for most buyers before sign-up. Treating it as a messaging and positioning asset changes how you write and maintain it.

S1
Spec Project · GTM Planning · Launch Strategy
GTM Launch Brief: peopleHum — Entering the Mid-Market HRMS Space
Independent project based on work at peopleHum
Spec Work
GTM Framework
+
What this is

At peopleHum, I owned SEO and content strategy — not GTM planning. But I was close enough to the product and market to develop this GTM framework independently. It reflects how I'd approach a launch brief if I owned it as a PMM.

The Brief

Product challenge

peopleHum is a full-suite HRMS competing in a market where Darwinbox, Keka, and GreytHR have strong brand recognition. The challenge: peopleHum's AI-led features are genuinely differentiated, but the messaging blends into the category noise.

Target segment for this launch

HR Directors at Indian companies, 100–500 employees, moving off legacy tools. Motivated by compliance pressure, remote team management, and the need to demonstrate HR ROI to leadership.

Positioning angle

Core differentiator

"The HRMS that shows HR's impact, not just HR's activity." Position around outcome visibility — helping HR teams prove their ROI to business leaders — rather than feature breadth.

This shifts the buyer conversation from "which HRMS has more features" to "which HRMS helps HR teams become strategic partners." A frame that Darwinbox and Keka don't own.

GTM Plan (Abbreviated)
Phase 1 — Awareness (Weeks 1–4)

Thought leadership content targeting "HR ROI" and "people analytics" keywords. LinkedIn campaign targeting HR Directors at target company size. Goal: 500 MQLs.

Phase 2 — Consideration (Weeks 5–8)

Competitor comparison pages (vs. Darwinbox, vs. Keka). Customer case study featuring measurable HR ROI outcome. Demo webinar series. Goal: 20% MQL-to-demo conversion.

Phase 3 — Conversion (Weeks 9–12)

Sales battle card for Darwinbox deals. ROI calculator tool on pricing page. Free trial with guided onboarding. Goal: 40 new logo conversions.

Success Metrics
  • Leading indicators: MQL volume from target ICP, demo booking rate.
  • Content metrics: Organic traffic to comparison pages, SERP ranking movement for target keywords, LLM citation rate for "best HRMS India" queries.
